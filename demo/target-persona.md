# Target Persona

## Current State
The primary buyer is an **Operations or People lead at a 150–500 person hybrid company** — typically someone with "Head of Ops", "VP of People", or "Chief of Staff" in their title. They manage internal communication rhythms and are accountable for keeping distributed teams aligned without multiplying meetings. They have budget authority or can easily get sign-off, and they feel the alignment overhead problem acutely because they're the ones who end up scheduling the syncs everyone else resents.

The end user — the person who records check-ins day to day — is an individual contributor or team lead. They're not the buyer. The buyer cares about reducing meeting load and improving async norms; the end user cares about whether it takes less than 90 seconds to complete a check-in.

We are not primarily targeting remote-first startups. Early assumptions pointed there, but discovery interviews showed that fully remote startups have already solved this with culture and tooling. The acute pain is in companies that recently went hybrid and haven't rebuilt their communication infrastructure.
