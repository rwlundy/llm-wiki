# Market Assumptions

## Current State
The competitive landscape is more complex than initially understood. Early framing positioned Meridian against **Loom** (async video) and **Zoom/Google Meet** (synchronous meetings). This framing was too narrow.

The more accurate competitive set is:

- **Microsoft Loop + Teams** — the most significant emerging threat. Enterprise companies already paying for Microsoft 365 are being offered Loop (structured async collaboration) and Teams async features as part of their existing contract. The cost of switching to Meridian for these companies is high; the cost of staying is zero.
- **Loom** — direct competitor on async video, but Loom is moving upmarket and becoming a sales/marketing tool rather than an internal collaboration tool. This may open space for Meridian.
- **Notion** — indirect competitor via "team wikis" and docs, but not async-first.
- **Status-update Slack bots** (Geekbot, Range, Parabol) — the most direct functional competitors. Low cost, already inside Slack, require no new habit. Underestimated in initial analysis.

**Willingness to pay** has been revised downward. Initial assumption was $15–25/user/month based on comparables in the project management space. Discovery interviews suggest $8–12/user/month is more realistic for a standalone async tool with no deep integrations. Enterprise buyers are harder to close than expected; they want SSO, audit logs, and admin controls before signing.

The total addressable market estimate of 2M+ companies globally in the 50–500 seat range remains plausible but has not been independently validated. Treat it as directional.

## Confidence
**Level:** Medium
**Reason:** Competitive framing has shifted twice. Willingness to pay assumptions are based on 14 interviews, not live pricing experiments. The Microsoft Loop threat is real but its adoption rate in our target segment is unclear. Several assumptions remain untested.

## Open Questions
- What is the actual adoption rate of Microsoft Loop and Teams async features in the 150–500 person hybrid company segment? If it's >30%, the enterprise sales motion may be structurally difficult.
- Is the Slack bot competitor set a meaningful threat or a sign that the problem is too lightweight to warrant a standalone product?
- Should we pilot a lower price point ($6–8/user/month) to accelerate adoption, or hold at $10–12 to protect perceived value?
- Is there a segment of companies that are committed to avoiding Microsoft products where Meridian has structural advantage?

## Revision Log
*(newest at top)*

### 2026-03-15 — Microsoft Loop identified as primary strategic threat
Two enterprise sales conversations (companies of 220 and 380 people) both raised Microsoft Loop unprompted. Both are on Microsoft 365 enterprise contracts. Loop is positioned as included-in-contract, reducing the perceived value of a standalone product. Competitive framing updated to elevate Microsoft as primary threat. Willingness to pay assumptions remain at $8–12/user/month; no change.

### 2026-02-20 — Willingness to pay revised down; Slack bot competitors added to competitive set
Five additional discovery interviews focused on pricing. $15–25/user/month was met with significant resistance. Revised range: $8–12/user/month for SMB, $12–15 possible for enterprise if SSO and audit logs are included. Geekbot, Range, and Parabol added to competitive analysis — previously overlooked because they're bots rather than standalone products, but functionally they solve the same core problem.

### 2026-01-05 — Loom competitive framing updated
Loom's 2025 product updates showed a clear move toward sales enablement and external video (demos, proposals, customer comms). Internal async collaboration is no longer their primary focus. This reduces direct competitive pressure from Loom and may open the market for Meridian in the internal comms space.

### 2025-11-30 — Initial competitive analysis revised; Notion added
Original competitive set (Loom, Zoom, Google Meet) reviewed. Notion added as an indirect competitor — teams using Notion for async updates and status docs are a meaningful alternative behaviour, even if the tool isn't designed for it. Willingness to pay research initiated; no data yet.

### 2025-10-15 — Initial market assumptions established
Competitive set: Loom (async video), Zoom/Google Meet (sync meetings). TAM estimated at 2M+ companies globally in the 50–500 seat range. Willingness to pay assumed at $15–25/user/month based on project management SaaS comparables. No independent validation.
