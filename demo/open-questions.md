# Open Questions

## Current State
A set of strategic questions that remain genuinely unresolved as of April 2026. These are not things we've overlooked — they are decisions we've deliberately deferred because we don't yet have enough information to answer them well. They should be revisited as data becomes available.

**Go-to-market motion: PLG vs. enterprise-led**
Do we grow bottom-up (individual teams adopt Meridian, usage spreads, companies eventually buy a team plan) or top-down (target Ops/People leads directly, sell seats, manage a traditional B2B sales process)? The two motions require different products, different pricing structures, different marketing, and different hiring. We have not committed to either. Current instinct leans PLG, but our identified buyer persona (Ops lead with budget) suggests enterprise-led may convert better in the short term.

**Pricing model**
We have a working assumption of $10/user/month but have not run any live pricing experiments. The right model may be per-seat, per-team, per-prompt-cycle, or usage-based. We don't know yet. This cannot be determined without a live product and paying customers.

**Mobile vs. desktop-first**
Check-in recordings are most natural on mobile (you're between meetings, you have 90 seconds, you record on your phone). But the admin dashboard and prompt scheduling workflow are desktop tasks. We have not decided which platform to prioritise for the initial build. Current engineering assumption is web-first, responsive design — but this may result in a mediocre experience on both rather than an excellent one on either.

**Default response visibility**
Should check-in responses be visible to the whole team by default, or visible to the direct manager only? Open visibility builds team transparency but creates privacy risk and may suppress honest responses. Manager-only visibility is safer but reduces the "team ritual" feel. No decision made.

**Enterprise vs. SMB as initial target**
Discovery interviews covered both segments. Enterprise deals are larger but slower and require features (SSO, audit logs, procurement compliance) that are not built. SMB deals are faster but may not justify the cost of a B2B sales motion. Initial focus is unclear.
